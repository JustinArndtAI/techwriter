🔐 Account & Access

I forgot my password. How do I reset it?

Quick Solution:

  1. Go to the ZipFlow login page
  2. Click "Forgot password?" below the password field
  3. Enter your work email address
  4. Check your email for a reset link (arrives within 2-3 minutes)
  5. Click the link and create a new password

Password Requirements:

  • At least 12 characters long
  • Must include: uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters
  • Cannot reuse your last 5 passwords
  • Cannot contain your name or common dictionary words

Still not working?

Your account may be locked after multiple failed attempts. Contact your IT administrator or our support team.

Why can't I see certain budget codes or departments in the dropdown?

Most Common Causes:

  • Permissions: Your user role may not have access to those budget codes
  • Department Restrictions: Some codes are restricted to specific departments
  • Inactive Status: Budget codes may be temporarily disabled

How to Fix:

  1. Check with your manager - they can request additional permissions
  2. Contact your ZipFlow administrator - they control budget code access
  3. Verify your department assignment in your profile settings

Need immediate access?

Your administrator can grant temporary permissions for urgent requests.

I'm getting a "Permission Denied" error when trying to approve requests. What's wrong?

Check These Common Issues:

Role Assignment:

  • Verify you're assigned as an approver in the workflow settings
  • Some approvals require specific job titles or seniority levels

Delegation Status:

  • If you're covering for someone, ensure proper delegation is set up
  • Check if approval delegation has expired

Budget Thresholds:

  • Certain amounts may require higher-level approval authority
  • Your approval limit may be lower than the request amount

Quick Fix:

Go to Settings > My Profile > Approval Authority to verify your current limits and delegations.

How do I change my notification preferences?

Step-by-Step:

  1. Click your profile picture (top-right corner)
  2. Select "Settings" from the dropdown
  3. Navigate to "Notifications" in the left sidebar
  4. Choose your preferences:
    • Email: Approval requests, status updates, budget alerts
    • In-App: Real-time notifications, urgent requests
    • SMS: Emergency approvals only (optional)
    • Frequency: Immediate, hourly digest, or daily summary

Recommended Settings for New Users:

  • Email: All approval-related notifications
  • In-App: Status changes and comments
  • SMS: Only for requests over $5,000

Why am I seeing requests from other departments in my dashboard?

Possible Reasons:

Cross-Department Role:

  • You may be assigned as a backup approver for multiple departments
  • Some roles (Finance, Procurement) see all requests by design

Shared Cost Centers:

  • Your department may share budget codes with other teams
  • Project-based requests often span multiple departments

Administrative Access:

Super admin or procurement manager roles have broad visibility

To Customize Your View:

  1. Use the department filter in your dashboard
  2. Set up custom views for your primary responsibilities
  3. Contact support to adjust your role permissions if needed

📝 Purchase Process

My request has been "pending approval" for over a week. What should I do?

First, Check the Workflow Status:

  1. Go to Requests > My Requests
  2. Click on your request ID
  3. Review the approval timeline - see exactly which step it's stuck at

Common Reasons for Delays:

Manager Out of Office:

  • Check if your approver has set up delegation
  • Look for "Out of Office" indicators in the workflow view

Missing Information:

  • Review any comments or requests for additional details
  • Check if supporting documents were properly uploaded

Budget Hold:

  • End-of-quarter budget freezes may delay approvals
  • New fiscal year budget allocations can cause temporary delays

Escalation Steps:

Day 3 Send polite follow-up to your direct approver
Day 5 Contact the next level approver or your manager
Day 7 Escalate through ZipFlow support for process review

Urgent Requests:

Use the "High Priority" flag when creating requests that need same-day approval.

Can I edit a request after it's been submitted?

The Short Answer:

It depends on the current approval status.

When You CAN Edit:

  • ✅ Submitted but not yet reviewed - Full editing capability
  • ✅ Returned for more information - Edit requested sections only
  • ✅ On hold - Limited editing allowed

When You CANNOT Edit:

  • ❌ Currently being reviewed - Approver is actively working on it
  • ❌ Already approved - Request is locked to maintain audit trail
  • ❌ In procurement processing - Order may already be placed

How to Make Changes:

For Active Requests:

  1. Go to your request details page
  2. Click "Request Changes" button
  3. Add a comment explaining what needs to be updated
  4. Approvers will return it to you for modifications

For Approved Requests:

  • You'll need to create a new request for additional items
  • Or create a change request if it's for the same purchase

Best Practice:

Double-check all details before submitting to avoid delays in the approval process.

How do I create an urgent or emergency request?

When to Use Emergency Priority:

  • Safety equipment needs
  • Critical system failures requiring immediate replacement
  • Regulatory compliance deadlines
  • Revenue-impacting situations

How to Create Emergency Requests:

  1. Start a new request as normal
  2. Select "High Priority" in the urgency section
  3. Add detailed justification in the description:
    • What's the business impact?
    • Why can't this wait for normal processing?
    • When is the absolute deadline?
  4. Attach supporting documentation (incident reports, compliance notices, etc.)
  5. Submit and notify your manager directly via email or phone

Emergency Processing Timeline:

High Priority Same-day approval (within 4 hours)
Standard 2-3 business days
Low Priority 5-7 business days

Important:

Misusing emergency priority may result in future restrictions on your account.

What's the difference between "goods," "services," "software," and "travel" request types?

Understanding request types ensures faster approvals and proper budget allocation:

Type Description Approval Flow Required Items Timeline
🛍️ Goods Office supplies, equipment, furniture, hardware Manager → Finance (if >$500) → Procurement Vendor quotes, delivery address, asset tagging info 3-7 business days
🤝 Services Consulting, maintenance contracts, professional services Manager → Legal (if new vendor) → Finance → Procurement Statement of work, contractor agreements, insurance certificates 5-10 business days
💻 Software SaaS subscriptions, software licenses, digital tools Manager → IT Security → Finance → Procurement Security review, license terms, integration requirements 7-14 business days (includes security review)
✈️ Travel Flights, hotels, rental cars, conference fees Manager → Travel Admin → Finance (if >$1000) Business justification, travel policy compliance 1-3 business days

Pro Tip:

Choosing the wrong category can add 2-5 days to your approval timeline.

How do I track my spending against my department budget?

Real-Time Budget Tracking:

Dashboard View:

  1. Your main dashboard shows "Budget Status" card
  2. Green = healthy, Yellow = caution (80%), Red = over budget (95%+)
  3. Click the card for detailed breakdown

Detailed Reports:

  1. Go to Reports > Department Spending
  2. Filter by date range, cost center, or category
  3. Export to Excel for detailed analysis

Budget Alerts Setup:

  1. Settings > Notifications > Budget Alerts
  2. Set thresholds: 75%, 85%, 95% of budget used
  3. Choose notification method: email, in-app, or both

Monthly Budget Calendar:

Week 1 25% of budget typically used
Week 2 50% of budget typically used
Week 3 75% of budget typically used
Week 4 Monitor closely for overspend risk

Budget Planning Tips:

  • Front-load annual purchases (Q1-Q2) for better vendor terms
  • Reserve 10-15% budget for emergency purchases
  • Track both committed (approved) and actual (spent) amounts

What should I do if I'm getting API rate limit errors?

Understanding Rate Limits:

ZipFlow API rate limits prevent system overload and ensure consistent performance for all users.

Current Limits:

  • Standard Users: 1,000 API calls per hour
  • Power Users: 5,000 API calls per hour
  • Enterprise: 15,000 API calls per hour
  • System Integrations: 50,000 API calls per hour

When Rate Limits Trigger:

  • Bulk data imports/exports
  • Automated reporting systems
  • Third-party integrations with high call volume
  • Development/testing environments making repeated calls

Solutions:

Immediate (Temporary Fix):

  1. Wait 60 minutes for rate limit reset
  2. Reduce API call frequency in your applications
  3. Implement caching to reduce duplicate requests

Long-term (Permanent Fix):

  1. Optimize API Usage: Batch requests when possible
  2. Implement Exponential Backoff: Retry failed requests with increasing delays
  3. Use Webhooks: Receive real-time updates instead of polling
  4. Request Rate Limit Increase: Contact support for enterprise-level limits

Best Practices:

  • Cache frequently accessed data locally
  • Use bulk endpoints for multiple record operations
  • Monitor your API usage in the developer dashboard

How do I set up single sign-on (SSO) with our corporate directory?

SSO Setup Requirements:

Supported Identity Providers:

  • Active Directory (ADFS)
  • Azure Active Directory
  • Google Workspace
  • Okta
  • OneLogin
  • PingIdentity
  • Generic SAML 2.0 providers

Prerequisites:

  • Administrator access to your identity provider
  • ZipFlow Enterprise plan (SSO not available on basic plans)
  • IT security approval for integrating new applications
  • Domain verification to confirm organization ownership

Setup Process:

Phase 1: Planning (1-2 days)

  1. Identify which users need ZipFlow access
  2. Determine user groups and permission levels
  3. Plan user attribute mapping (name, email, department, role)

Phase 2: Configuration (3-5 days)

  1. Contact ZipFlow support to initiate SSO setup
  2. Provide metadata from your identity provider
  3. Configure user attribute mappings
  4. Set up test users for validation

Phase 3: Testing (2-3 days)

  1. Test login with pilot user group
  2. Verify user provisioning and role assignments
  3. Test user deprovisioning when employees leave

Phase 4: Rollout (1 day)

  1. Communicate changes to all users
  2. Provide backup authentication methods
  3. Monitor for login issues during transition

Ongoing Maintenance:

  • User provisioning/deprovisioning happens automatically
  • Group membership changes sync every 4 hours
  • Contact support for attribute mapping changes

How do I generate reports for budget variance analysis?

Budget Variance Reporting Made Simple:

Quick Variance Dashboard:

  1. Navigate to Reports > Budget Analysis
  2. Select your department or cost center
  3. Choose comparison period (month-over-month, year-over-year)
  4. View automatic variance calculations with visual indicators

Detailed Variance Report Setup Steps:

  1. Go to Reports > Custom Report Builder
  2. Select Report Type: "Budget vs. Actual Analysis"
  3. Choose Parameters:
    • Time period (monthly, quarterly, annually)
    • Organizational levels (department, cost center, project)
    • Variance thresholds (5%, 10%, 15%)
  4. Add Filters: Category, vendor, approval status
  5. Generate Report: Preview before finalizing

Report Components:

  • Budget Allocated: Original budget amounts by category
  • Actual Spent: Confirmed expenditures to date
  • Committed: Approved but not yet spent amounts
  • Available: Remaining budget capacity
  • Variance %: Positive/negative deviation from budget
  • Trend Analysis: Spending patterns over time

Key Variance Metrics:

Favorable Variances (Under Budget):

  • Indicates potential cost savings opportunities
  • May suggest conservative budgeting
  • Could reveal delayed projects or initiatives

Unfavorable Variances (Over Budget):

  • Requires immediate attention and explanation
  • May indicate budget planning issues
  • Could signal process control problems

Automated Alerts:

Set up variance alerts for:

  • 5% Variance: Early warning notification
  • 10% Variance: Manager notification required
  • 15% Variance: Executive escalation triggered

Best Practice Reports:

  1. Monthly Budget Reviews: Track progress against annual budgets
  2. Quarterly Variance Analysis: Detailed category-level review
  3. Year-End Reconciliation: Final budget vs. actual comparison
  4. Rolling 12-Month Trends: Identify seasonal patterns

Export Options:

  • Excel format with pivot tables for further analysis
  • PDF executive summary for management presentations
  • CSV data for integration with business intelligence tools

How do I set up automated approval workflows for recurring purchases?

Streamline Repetitive Approvals:

When to Use Automated Workflows:

  • Monthly software subscriptions
  • Quarterly office supply orders
  • Annual maintenance contracts
  • Regular utility and service payments
  • Scheduled equipment replacements

Setup Process:

Step 1: Identify Recurring Patterns

  1. Go to Reports > Purchase Analysis
  2. Filter by "Recurring Purchases"
  3. Identify patterns: Same vendor, similar amounts, regular frequency
  4. Document business justification for automation

Step 2: Configure Automation Rules

  1. Navigate to Settings > Workflow Automation
  2. Click "Create New Automated Workflow"
  3. Define Triggers:
    • Vendor matches approved list
    • Amount within predetermined range (±10%)
    • Purchase category matches template
    • Department and cost center alignment
  4. Set Conditions:
    • Budget availability check
    • Spending limit compliance
    • Vendor contract compliance
    • Policy adherence verification

Step 3: Design Approval Path

  1. Choose Approval Type:
    • Auto-Approve: No human intervention required
    • Express Approval: 4-hour automatic approval if no objection
    • Simplified Approval: Single approver instead of full chain
  2. Set Notification Rules:
    • Who gets notified about auto-approved purchases
    • Escalation if automation rules fail
    • Weekly/monthly summary reports

Advanced Automation Features:

Smart Templates:

  • Pre-populate request forms with historical data
  • Automatic vendor selection based on past performance
  • Budget code assignment based on request type

Conditional Logic:

  • If amount > $X, require additional approval
  • If new vendor, bypass automation and require manual review
  • If budget remaining < Y%, escalate to department head

Exception Handling:

  • Automatic fallback to manual approval if criteria not met
  • Alert notifications for unusual purchase patterns
  • Override capabilities for urgent business needs

Monitoring and Optimization:

Performance Metrics:

  • Percentage of purchases processed via automation
  • Time savings compared to manual processing
  • Accuracy rates and exception frequency
  • User satisfaction and adoption rates

Regular Review Process:

  1. Monthly: Review automation performance and exceptions
  2. Quarterly: Adjust dollar thresholds and approval criteria
  3. Annually: Comprehensive workflow optimization review

Risk Management:

  • Maximum automation limits by category
  • Required periodic human review (quarterly/annually)
  • Audit trail maintenance for all automated decisions
  • Control testing for compliance requirements

Getting Started:

Contact your ZipFlow Customer Success Manager to:

  • Analyze your purchase patterns for automation opportunities
  • Configure pilot automation workflows
  • Provide training on automation management
  • Establish monitoring and optimization processes
